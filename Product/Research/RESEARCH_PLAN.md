## 3. Willingness to Pay Research

### Objective
Validate assumption B3: "Schools/parents will pay $X per student/year"

### Survey Design

**Target Respondents**:
- 50 parents of high school students in UAE/Saudi
- 30 high school students (16-18)
- 20 school counselors/administrators

**Key Questions**:
1. How much do you currently spend on career guidance/test prep?
2. Would you pay for a comprehensive career exploration platform?
3. At what price point? ($5/month, $10/month, $50/year, $100/year)
4. What features would justify payment?
5. Prefer subscription or one-time payment?

**Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity**:
- At what price is this too cheap (low quality)?
- At what price is this a bargain?
- At what price is this getting expensive?
- At what price is this too expensive?

### Distribution Channels
- Parent WhatsApp/Facebook groups
- School counselor networks
- Survey incentives ($5 gift card)

### Deliverable
Pricing recommendation with confidence intervals

## 4. User Problem Validation

### Objective
Validate assumptions P1-P5: Problem exists and is significant

### Interview Plan

**Students (n=10)**:
- Grade 10-12, mix of public/private schools
- UAE (5), Saudi (5)
- Mix of genders

**Parents (n=10)**:
- Parents of high school students
- Mix of expat/local
- Mix of income levels

**School Counselors (n=5)**:
- From private/international schools
- UAE (3), Saudi (2)

### Interview Guide Topics

**Students**:
1. How do you think about your future career?
2. What resources have you used to explore careers?
3. How did you choose (or plan to choose) your university major?
4. What's confusing or difficult about career decisions?
5. How does your family influence your choices?

**Parents**:
1. How involved are you in your child's career/education decisions?
2. What concerns do you have about their future career?
3. What resources have you sought out?
4. What's missing from current options?
5. How do you balance child's interests vs. practical concerns?

**Counselors**:
1. What tools do you use for career guidance?
2. What's the biggest gap in resources available?
3. How do students typically make career/major decisions?
4. What would an ideal tool look like?
5. How are budget decisions made for tools like this?

### Deliverable
User research synthesis with key insights and quotes

## 5. Content Gap Analysis

### Objective
Validate assumption S1: "761 careers provides sufficient coverage"

### Analysis Steps

1. **MENA Job Market Comparison**
   - Top 50 jobs in demand in Saudi Arabia
   - Top 50 jobs in demand in UAE
   - Compare against our 761 careers
   - Identify gaps

2. **Student Interest Coverage**
   - What careers do MENA students commonly ask about?
   - Are emerging careers (AI, sustainability) covered?
   - Are MENA-specific careers covered (Islamic finance, Arabic media)?

3. **Major-Career Completeness**
   - Do all majors link to relevant careers?
   - Are career-major relationships accurate?

4. **Arabic Content Quality**
   - What % of careers have Arabic translations?
   - Quality audit of Arabic content (native speaker review)

### Deliverable
Content gap report with prioritized additions

## 6. School Budget Research

### Objective
Understand school purchasing process and budget cycles

### Interview Guide (School Administrators)

1. What is your annual budget for EdTech tools?
2. How are purchasing decisions made?
3. What time of year do you evaluate new tools?
4. What criteria matter most (price, features, support)?
5. Who needs to approve purchases?
6. What's your typical contract length?

### Target
5 school administrators/principals from private schools in UAE/Saudi

### Deliverable
B2B sales playbook with timing and decision-maker insights
