## Primary Personas

### Persona 1: Sarah - The Uncertain Graduate

**Type:** B2C End User
**Segment:** Fresh Graduates

**Background:**
- Age: 22-25
- Just completed university degree
- First job or job-seeking
- Made career/major choice at 15 without guidance

**Story:**
> "At the tender age of 15, she stood at a crossroads. She chose to immerse herself in the world of Literature, a choice driven more by circumstance than careful thought... Six precious years of her life had been spent chasing after a goal that wasn't truly hers."

**Goals:**
- Explore different career paths
- Understand skills needed to succeed
- Connect with mentors/professionals
- Track progress and set career goals

**Pain Points:**
- Not sure what kind of job they want
- Made uninformed decisions as a teenager
- Feels disconnect between education and career aspirations
- Lacks access to career information and guidance

**Desired Features:**
- Career path exploration
- Job recommendations based on interests
- Mentor connections
- Progress tracking

**Quote:** "I'm living a life that was decided by a 15-year-old version of myself."

---

### Persona 2: Ahmad's Parents - The Concerned Parents

**Type:** B2C Payer
**Segment:** Parents of High School Students

**Background:**
- Parents of students aged 14-20
- Urban, middle-to-upper class
- Both parents often work full-time
- Highly invested in child's success

**Story:**
Parents face overwhelming uncertainty about how to guide their child's career decisions. They struggle with rapidly changing job market and lack access to reliable, updated career information.

**Goals:**
- Provide best opportunities for their child
- Guide informed career/major decisions
- Reduce stress and uncertainty about child's future
- Have meaningful conversations with child about careers

**Pain Points:**
- Lack of reliable career information
- Job market constantly changing
- Don't know how to guide career decisions
- Feel overwhelmed by options
- Lack time to research
- Traditional resources are outdated
- Feel guilt if child makes wrong choice

**Triggers to Search:**
- Child finishing high school
- Child entering university
- Need to choose major/track
- Career-related challenges arise

**Desired Outcomes:**
- Clear understanding of career options
- Improved communication with child
- Reduced stress and anxiety
- Confidence child is on right track

**Research Behavior:**
- Word of mouth from other parents
- Online search engines
- Education/career-focused articles
- Parent forums and WhatsApp groups

**Buying Confidence Factors:**
- Recommendations from trusted sources
- Positive reviews and testimonials
- Clear understanding of benefits
- Personalized approach
- Money-back guarantee

**Quote:** "Is my daughter in trouble? No? Then I'm not coming." (from counselor interview)

### Persona 3: Kamal - The Overloaded School Counselor

**Type:** B2B End User
**Segment:** School Counselors (K-12 Private Schools)

**Background:**
- Works at private school in Jordan/MENA
- Mid-career professional
- Handles 4-5 student sessions per week
- Peak workload at semester start/end

**Story:**
Kamal manages multiple responsibilities: individual sessions, counseling classes, group sessions, and administrative work. He relies on books, YouTube, and colleagues for content - no dedicated career platform.

**Goals:**
- Help students navigate school challenges
- Build relationships with students and teachers
- Meet administrative requirements
- Provide career guidance (secondary)

**Pain Points:**
- Students referred unwillingly don't engage
- Admin requests conflict with ethics
- No systematic career guidance resources
- Too many sessions, not enough time
- Hard to coordinate schedules
- Confidentiality concerns

**Current Tools:**
- WhatsApp groups
- Facebook
- YouTube for content
- Arabic psychology books

**Success Metrics Used:**
- Student grades
- Progress in case
- Student feedback
- Administrative compliance

**Desired Features:**
- Student self-service (reduce burden)
- Scheduling integration
- Progress tracking
- Arabic content
- Admin reports

**Quote:** "Building relationships with students is so important."

### Persona 4: Mariana - The Secondary School Leader

**Type:** B2B Decision Influencer
**Segment:** School Administrators

**Background:**
- Head of Secondary (10-12th grade)
- Private school in Egypt
- 20+ years experience
- BA in Sociology

**Story:**
Mariana balances teaching, administration, and student support. She wants to develop student personalities but faces resistance from parents who only engage when there's a problem.

**Goals:**
- Develop student personalities
- Train teachers on adolescent communication
- Meet ministry technology mandates
- Maintain discipline and control

**Pain Points:**
- Parents don't prioritize counseling
- Heavy administrative burden
- Balancing teaching and admin
- Technology implementation pressure
- Teachers older than her need convincing

**Current Tools:**
- SCL (Learning Management System)
- PowerPoint, Google Forms
- YouTube
- WhatsApp

**Influences:**
- Dr. Mohamed Taha
- Dr. Yehia Al-Rakhawi
- Psychology/counseling influencers

**Quote:** "Is my daughter in trouble? No? Then I'm not coming."

### Persona 5: Dr. Sulaiman - The University Counselor

**Type:** B2B End User
**Segment:** University Counseling Centers

**Background:**
- PhD in Education (Mental Health/Psychology)
- 13 years experience
- Public university in Saudi Arabia
- Provides psychological, academic, professional counseling

**Story:**
Dr. Sulaiman works with struggling university students, providing workshops and individual counseling. His biggest challenge is overcoming stigma - students don't see value in guidance.

**Goals:**
- Support struggling students
- Run training workshops
- Provide professional counseling
- Meet quarterly supervision requirements

**Pain Points:**
- Stigma around seeking help
- Students don't follow through on action plans
- Scheduling difficulties
- Language barriers (wants English resources)

**Current Tools:**
- Email-based communication
- Internal university systems
- Academic resources

**Desired Features:**
- Resources to reduce stigma
- Progress tracking
- Student follow-through tools
- English language content

**Quote:** "Some students are not convinced of guidance (stigma)."

---

### Persona 6: Ahmed - The Mid-Career Professional

**Type:** B2C End User
**Segment:** Mid-career Professionals

**Background:**
- Age: 30-45
- 5-15 years in workforce
- Considering career change or advancement
- Has skills and experience but seeking direction

**Story:**
Ahmed wants to explore new opportunities and understand how his skills translate to different careers. He seeks clarity and confidence in career decisions.

**Goals:**
- Explore new career opportunities
- Understand skills and strengths
- Make informed decisions about career path
- Achieve long-term professional goals

**Pain Points:**
- Unsure how skills transfer to other careers
- Lacks clarity on career direction
- Limited networking in new fields
- Needs personalized development recommendations

**Desired Features:**
- Skills/interests assessments
- Job recommendations based on results
- Professional networking
- Learning/development recommendations
- Progress tracking over time

**Quote:** "I want to gain greater clarity and confidence in my career decisions."

## Jobs To Be Done (JTBD)

### Students
1. When I'm choosing my major, I want to understand what careers it leads to, so I don't waste years studying something irrelevant
2. When I'm feeling lost about my future, I want to explore options that match my interests, so I feel confident in my direction
3. When I'm preparing for the job market, I want to know what skills employers need, so I can develop them now

### Parents
1. When my child is entering high school, I want reliable career information, so I can guide them toward good decisions
2. When I'm worried about my child's future, I want a clear action plan, so I feel confident they're on the right track
3. When I need to discuss careers with my child, I want conversation tools and shared information, so we can make decisions together

### Counselors
1. When I'm meeting with a student, I want quick access to career information, so I can provide helpful guidance
2. When I'm preparing counseling sessions, I want ready-made content, so I don't have to create everything from scratch
3. When I need to demonstrate value, I want progress reports, so I can show outcomes to administration

### Schools
1. When we're evaluating EdTech tools, we want proven outcomes, so we can justify the investment
2. When we're meeting ministry requirements, we want compliance features, so we pass audits
3. When parents ask about career guidance, we want to show our commitment, so they choose our school
